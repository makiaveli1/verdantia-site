## The Hidden Leak in Social Video Campaigns

AI tools make it faster to create short-form video ad ideas, hooks, scripts, captions, and visual directions for platforms such as TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and LinkedIn.

That speed is useful. It can also create a very expensive mess.

If the video hook promises one thing and the landing page delivers another, clicks turn into confusion. The audience may like the creative, but they do not understand the next step, the offer, or whether the promise is actually for them.

## Why Video-to-Page Coherence Matters

A social video ad is not just “creative”. It is a promise compressed into a few seconds.

The hook sets the expectation. The caption reinforces it. The landing page has to carry it through with the same audience, offer, proof, and call to action.

If your video says “get a free setup check” but the landing page asks for a vague “strategy consultation”, visitors feel the shift. If your Reel says “zero setup fee” but the checkout only shows paid onboarding packages, trust drops. If your LinkedIn video speaks to early-stage creators but the page only talks to enterprise buyers, the wrong people click and bounce.

These are common, preventable, and costly.

## Real-World Examples

Here are three common scenarios — and how a coherence check fixes them:

- **The Hook Mismatch**
  - Video: “Get your campaign live in 30 minutes with a free setup check.”
  - Landing Page: “Schedule a strategy consultation to discuss your goals.”
  - Fix: Make the page CTA match the video promise, or rewrite the hook so it reflects the actual next step.

- **The Fee Surprise**
  - Reel: “Launch your first campaign with zero setup fee.”
  - Landing Page: Checkout form only shows paid onboarding packages.
  - Fix: Add a clear zero-fee route or remove the claim from the video and caption.

- **The Audience Split**
  - LinkedIn Video: “Built for early-stage creators.”
  - Landing Page: Speaks only to enterprise buyers with complex procurement needs.
  - Fix: Tailor the page headline and proof to creators, or create separate assets/pages for each audience.
