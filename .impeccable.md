# Verdantia Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Verdantia serves AI-curious but practical buyers: Ireland/UK SME leaders, smaller corporate teams, training providers, nonprofit/community organisations, and individual professionals who need usable AI capability without hype. They arrive with uneven confidence, informal tool use, unclear risk boundaries, and a need to understand what to do next.

### Brand Personality
Founder-led, grounded, premium, practical, warm. Verdantia should feel like a calm practical-AI field guide and workshop operating system: serious enough for organisational trust, human enough for training rooms, and clear enough for non-specialists.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use an editorial field-guide / premium workshop-studio language: warm ivory paper, evergreen ink, sage, ochre/gold accents, tactile materials, route maps, checklists, notebooks, botanical marks, and composed still-life assets. Borrow from PromptLayer only for editorial confidence and high signal-to-noise. Do not use neon AI, robots, neural blobs, fake dashboards, glassmorphism spam, generic SaaS panels, or purple/blue gradient AI slop.

### Design Principles
1. Every page needs one job and one dominant next step; secondary routes should not compete.
2. Use the full desktop canvas with asymmetry, editorial spreads, route boards, and material surfaces instead of centered PDF columns.
3. Keep real copy in the DOM; generated imagery is atmospheric support, never the message itself.
4. Make Verdantia tangible through workshop artefacts, route maps, leave-behinds, and decision aids rather than vague transformation language.
5. Motion must clarify flow and state, respect reduced motion, and avoid performance-heavy spectacle.
6. Preserve honest commercial boundaries: training/adoption first; automation downstream; mailto form prepares a draft, it does not fake-send.
