## Priority logic

- **SMEs / smaller corporate teams:** pitch the move from scattered AI use to safe, repeatable workflows.
- **Training providers:** pitch Verdantia/Gbemi as practical GenAI delivery capacity or workshop partner.
- **Nonprofits / community organisations:** pitch accessible AI literacy and workflow support for small teams with limited capacity.

## First outreach experiment

Do not contact all 30 at once.

1. Pick **3 from each segment**.
2. Find one likely decision-maker per organisation.
3. Verify the contact route and one truthful personalisation line.
4. Draft exact final text for that named recipient.
5. Get Gbemi/Oluwagbemi approval for the exact text, channel, and recipient before sending.
6. Send manually only after approval.
7. Track: sent date, response, objection, next action.
8. After 9–12 approved contacts, adjust the offer language.

- SMEs: LearnUpon, Poppulo, Phorest.
- Training providers: Irish Times Training, The Learning Rooms, Professional Development.
- Nonprofits: The Wheel, Carmichael, Volunteer Ireland.

These are not guaranteed fits. They are good first experiments because the angle is easy to explain and the buyer context is plausible.
