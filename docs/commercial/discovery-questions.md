# Verdantia discovery questions

[DRAFT — INTERNAL WORKING DOC]

Use these questions before recommending an offer. The aim is to qualify fit, choose the lightest useful first step, and avoid selling a sprint when a briefing will do.

## 1. Context

1. What prompted the AI conversation now?
2. Who is asking for support: leadership, HR/L&D, operations, IT, a team manager, or a training provider?
3. How many people would need support in the first phase?
4. Is this for one team, several teams, or an external learner/client group?
5. Is there a date or event driving urgency?

## 2. Current AI use

1. Which tools are people already using? ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, Perplexity, others?
2. Are any tools formally approved or blocked by the organisation?
3. Is usage visible and discussed, or mostly informal/shadow use?
4. What tasks are people already trying to improve with AI?
5. Where has AI been useful so far?
6. Where has it caused confusion, low-quality output, or risk?

## 3. Workflows and pain

1. Which work takes too long today?
2. Which repeated tasks create bottlenecks?
3. Which documents, meetings, reports, emails, or processes create the most friction?
4. What would be valuable if improved: speed, quality, consistency, confidence, compliance, training capacity?
5. What does success look like 30 days after the session?

## 4. Risk and governance

1. What data should never be pasted into external AI tools?
2. Are there existing policies for AI, data protection, confidentiality, or client information?
3. Who needs to approve safe-use guidance?
4. What mistakes would be costly or embarrassing?
5. Do participants need basic literacy first, or are they ready for workflow design?

## 5. Buyer and budget

1. Who owns the budget?
2. Who signs off the work?
3. Is this training budget, innovation budget, operations budget, or programme delivery budget?
4. Have they paid for similar training or consulting before?
5. Are they looking for a one-off session or an adoption path?

## 6. Recommended offer logic

- Choose **AI Team Briefing** if the team needs shared understanding, leadership buy-in, or a safe first conversation.
- Choose **Practical AI Workshop** if the team needs hands-on confidence and usable habits.
- Choose **AI Adoption Day** if the team already has interest and wants use-case mapping plus a starter roadmap.
- Choose **AI Adoption Sprint** if there are clear workflows, urgency, and a need for reusable materials/follow-up support.

## 7. Red flags

- “We just need AI” with no owner, audience, or business problem.
- No decision-maker or budget owner involved.
- Expecting automation/custom assistants before workflow clarity.
- Wanting proof of scale Verdantia cannot honestly claim yet.
- Needing regulated/legal/compliance guarantees outside current capability.

## 8. Green flags

- Specific team and use case.
- Clear decision-maker.
- Existing AI experimentation that needs structure.
- Training provider needing delivery capacity.
- Leadership cares about safe, practical adoption rather than hype.
