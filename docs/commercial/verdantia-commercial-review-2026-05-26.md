## The 1-3-1 decision

**Problem:** Verdantia needs a growth model that earns trust quickly while still being contractable, repeatable, and scalable beyond Gbemi’s personal LinkedIn presence.

### Option A — Pure personal brand / speaker-trainer

**Pros:** fastest to explain, authentic, low overhead, strong LinkedIn fit.
**Cons:** weaker for procurement, subcontractors, IP ownership, future retainers, and anything bigger than Gbemi’s calendar.

### Option B — Faceless company / consultancy brand

**Pros:** easier to make look “corporate”, potentially more scalable later.
**Cons:** hides the strongest early trust asset, risks fake-scale theatre, harder to differentiate without case studies.

### Option C — Founder-led company

**Pros:** buyers trust Gbemi, contracts live through Verdantia, materials/IP can compound, partners can engage professionally, future contractors can plug in.
**Cons:** requires discipline so Verdantia does not become vague brand wallpaper while Gbemi does all the real selling.

## Recommendation

Choose **Option C: founder-led company**. Verdantia should own the commercial system, offer ladder, materials, delivery standards, contracts, and follow-up. Gbemi should remain highly visible as the teaching/trust layer through LinkedIn, speaking, workshops, and field notes.
