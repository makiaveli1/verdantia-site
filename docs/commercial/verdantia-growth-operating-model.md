## Strategic decision

Verdantia should operate as a founder-led company, not as a personal brand only and not as a fake-large consultancy.

**Operating shorthand:** Verdantia is the vehicle. Gbemi is the trust engine.

- **Gbemi / Oluwagbemi** builds trust through LinkedIn, speaking, field notes, workshops, and practical teaching.
- **Verdantia** holds the commercial system: contracts, proposals, workshops, retainers, subcontractors, delivery standards, materials, and follow-up.

## Current positioning

Verdantia helps small and mid-sized teams move from scattered AI experiments to safe, practical, repeatable AI workflows.

Short version:

> Practical AI training and adoption support for teams.

Use both words deliberately:

- **AI training** is easier for buyers to understand.
- **AI adoption** signals the higher-value outcome: better workplace habits, workflows, confidence, and safe use.

Recommended public phrase:

> Practical AI training for teams that need to turn AI experiments into safe, useful workplace habits.

## Proof posture

Verdantia has real delivery proof, but should present it carefully without overclaiming scale.

Known proof, private-source only:

- Gbemi has delivered Microsoft Copilot and introductory AI training in public-sector, financial-services, and workplace contexts, including partner/co-delivery settings.
- Named organisations, partner routes, and permission status should live in private memory or a gitignored private proof register, not in public-facing or GitHub-backed commercial docs.

Public proof rule:

- Do **not** publicly name underlying client organisations.
- Use unnamed sector proof language: **"delivered Microsoft Copilot and introductory AI training for public-sector, financial-services, and workplace teams."**
- Internally, the named examples can inform confidence and positioning, but external copy must stay unnamed unless explicit future approval changes this.
- Do not imply Verdantia alone held every contract unless true.
- Do not imply case studies, testimonials, or official endorsements until written permission exists.

## Growth route

The first 30-day strategy is **both direct clients and partner/provider routes**.

### Route A — Direct clients

Target buyers:

- SMEs and smaller corporate teams.
- Teams with Microsoft 365 / Copilot access but weak adoption habits.
- Nonprofits and community organisations where accessible AI literacy creates proof and trust.

Primary offer:

- Practical AI Workshop.

Entry offer:

- AI Team Briefing.

Upsell:

- AI Adoption Day or AI Adoption Sprint.

### Route B — Training-provider / partner routes

Target partners:

- Training providers that need practical GenAI delivery capacity.
- Consultancies with client demand but no grounded AI-training specialist.
- Warm partner/provider routes where Gbemi can co-deliver or bring Verdantia material into a broader programme, subject to permission and commercial fit.

Primary pitch:

> I can deliver practical GenAI and Copilot training under your programme or as a specialist partner, with clear workplace exercises, responsible-use guidance, and a delivery reliability protocol.

Boundary:

- Partners can provide access and capacity.
- Verdantia must still own its core curriculum, delivery standards, and buyer-facing clarity.
- Do not become dependent on partner materials or partner-controlled client access.

## Decision rules

- If direct clients respond faster: push the workshop/briefing ladder.
- If partners respond faster: package Verdantia as specialist delivery capacity while still building direct assets.
- If both are slow: tighten the proof statement and outreach angle before expanding the list.
- If prospects ask for automation first: route them through discovery; do not sell builds before workflow/risk clarity.

## Non-negotiables

- No fake scale.
- No fake case studies.
- No generic AI automation agency positioning.
- No public named-client claims without approval.
- No external outreach or LinkedIn posting without exact-text approval.
- Every live workshop should have a delivery reliability protocol: offline PDF, preloaded tabs, screenshots/video backups, tech check, and framework-first fallback.
