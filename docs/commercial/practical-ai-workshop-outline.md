# Practical AI Workshop outline

[DRAFT — NOT FOR DISTRIBUTION — REQUIRES OLUWAGBEMI APPROVAL]

## Format

- **Duration:** Half day, 3–3.5 hours plus breaks
- **Audience:** Non-technical and mixed-skill workplace teams
- **Tools:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Microsoft Copilot, Perplexity, or the tools already approved by the organisation
- **Commercial guide:** €1,250–€1,750

## Workshop promise

By the end of the workshop, participants should understand where AI can help, where it can mislead them, how to prompt safely, and how to apply AI to real tasks with human review.

## Learning outcomes

Participants will be able to:

1. Match common AI tools to different work tasks.
2. Write clearer prompts using role, task, context, constraints, and output format.
3. Spot common failure modes: hallucination, weak sources, privacy risks, stale information, overconfident output.
4. Apply AI to realistic workplace examples.
5. Use a responsible-use checklist before sharing or acting on AI-assisted work.

## Session flow

### 1. Opening and baseline — 20 minutes

- What people already use AI for.
- What they are unsure about.
- The difference between AI as toy, assistant, search companion, writing partner, and workflow support.

### 2. Tool literacy — 35 minutes

- ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, Perplexity: where each tends to fit.
- Why “which AI is best?” is the wrong starting question.
- The safer question: “What work are we improving?”

### 3. Prompting that survives real work — 45 minutes

- Good prompt anatomy: role, task, context, constraints, examples, output format, review criteria.
- Bad prompt → better prompt exercise.
- How to ask for assumptions, risks, alternatives, and verification steps.

### 4. Role-based practice — 60 minutes

Pick 2–4 relevant lanes depending on audience:

- Admin and operations: summarising, drafting, checklists, process notes.
- Sales/marketing: first drafts, customer research, messaging options, campaign planning.
- Management: meeting prep, decision briefs, risk lists, internal communications.
- Training/education: lesson planning, learner support, assessment rubrics, feedback.
- Nonprofit/community: funding drafts, volunteer comms, event planning, reporting.

### 5. Safe-use habits — 35 minutes

- What not to paste into tools.
- When AI output needs source checking.
- Human review points.
- Bias, tone, privacy, and accountability.
- Team rules: what is allowed, discouraged, and forbidden.

### 6. Use-case capture and next steps — 30 minutes

- Participants identify useful use cases.
- Group ranks by value, risk, and ease.
- Verdantia recommends next step: briefing follow-up, adoption day, sprint, or no further work needed.

## Deliverables

- Workshop slide deck / PDF.
- Participant handout.
- Prompt practice worksheet.
- Responsible-use checklist.
- Use-case capture sheet.
- Recommended next-step summary.

## Facilitation notes

Keep the tone practical. Do not turn the session into a parade of tools. The win is better judgement, not “look at all these shiny buttons.”
