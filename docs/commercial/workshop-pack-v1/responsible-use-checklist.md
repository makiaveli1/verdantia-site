# Responsible-use checklist

Use this before sharing, relying on, or acting on AI-assisted work.

## 1. Data boundary check

Do not paste the following into public AI tools unless your organisation has explicitly approved the tool and use case:

- Client/customer data
- Employee or personal data
- Financial records
- Legal, medical, safeguarding, or HR material
- Passwords, API keys, access tokens, or credentials
- Confidential strategy, contracts, pricing, or procurement information
- Unpublished intellectual property
- Anything you would not comfortably put into an external website

If in doubt, use synthetic, anonymised, or approved sample data.

## 2. Accuracy check

Before using output, check:

- [ ] Are names, dates, numbers, links, and facts correct?
- [ ] Did the AI invent sources, policies, or statistics?
- [ ] Does this need a current source or internal document check?
- [ ] Is any answer based on assumptions rather than evidence?
- [ ] Has a human with context reviewed it?

## 3. Context check

AI often answers the question you asked, not the question you meant.

- [ ] Did I give enough context?
- [ ] Does the output fit our audience?
- [ ] Does it reflect our actual process/policy?
- [ ] Are there missing constraints the AI could not know?
- [ ] Would someone misunderstand this if sent as-is?

## 4. Tone and judgement check

- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the audience?
- [ ] Is it too confident, too casual, or too generic?
- [ ] Could it sound insensitive, biased, dismissive, or unfair?
- [ ] Does it preserve the human relationship involved?
- [ ] Would I stand over this if challenged?

## 5. Decision-risk check

AI output should not be the sole basis for decisions involving:

- Hiring, firing, discipline, performance, or HR judgement
- Legal advice or contractual interpretation
- Medical, safeguarding, or welfare decisions
- Financial advice or high-stakes spending
- Security, privacy, or compliance decisions
- Anything that materially affects a person’s rights, access, or wellbeing

Use AI for drafting, structuring, summarising, and exploring options. Keep accountability with the human or team responsible.

## 7. Team habit

Before using AI output externally, ask:

> What would need to be true for this output to be safe, accurate, and useful?

Then verify those things. Tiny step, massive difference. Boring? Yes. Useful? Also yes. The annoying vegetables of AI adoption.
