## 2. Why now?

1. What prompted the AI conversation now?
2. What would make this session valuable for the organisation?
3. Is there a deadline, rollout, tool purchase, event, or leadership priority behind the timing?
4. What should participants be able to do differently 30 days after the session?

## 3. Current AI use

1. Which tools are already approved or available? Microsoft Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, others?
2. Which tools are blocked or discouraged?
3. Is current AI use visible and discussed, or mostly informal/shadow use?
4. What tasks are people already using AI for?
5. Where has AI been useful?
6. Where has it caused confusion, poor quality, privacy concern, or risk?

## 4. Data and safety boundaries

1. What data must never be entered into public AI tools?
2. Are there existing AI, data protection, confidentiality, or client-information policies?
3. Can exercises use live tools, or should Verdantia use prepared examples only?
4. Are there any regulatory, safeguarding, accessibility, or sector-specific concerns?
5. Who should approve safe-use guidance before the session?

## 6. Success criteria

By the end of the workshop, success looks like:

- [ ] Participants understand what AI can and cannot do.
- [ ] Participants can write better prompts for real work.
- [ ] Participants know what not to paste into AI tools.
- [ ] Participants can review AI output before using it.
- [ ] The team has a shortlist of practical use cases.
- [ ] Leadership/sponsor has a sensible next step.
- [ ] Other:

## 7. Commercial and follow-up fit

1. Is this intended as a one-off session or the start of a broader adoption path?
2. Who owns budget for follow-up work if useful?
3. Would a Workshop Follow-On Debrief be useful after the session?
4. If priority workflows emerge, would an Adoption Day or Sprint be considered?
