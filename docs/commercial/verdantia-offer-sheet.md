## Positioning

Verdantia helps small and mid-sized teams move from scattered AI experiments to safer, practical, repeatable AI working habits.

**Short version:** Practical AI training and adoption support for teams.

**Founder/company frame:** Verdantia is the commercial vehicle. Gbemi Akadiri is the visible trust engine: trainer, speaker, practitioner, and adoption guide.

## Best fit right now

Teams of roughly 10–50 people where AI use is already happening informally through tools like Microsoft Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or Perplexity — but there is no shared baseline for safe use, quality review, prompting, privacy, or repeatable workflows.

Especially useful for operations, admin, customer support, marketing, internal communications, L&D, training, nonprofit, and management teams that need practical habits rather than another abstract AI talk.

## Flagship starting point

The flagship early product is the **Practical AI Workshop**. Most teams do not need a large AI programme first. They need a half-day session that gives people shared language, safer prompting habits, real examples, review discipline, and a shortlist of use cases worth testing.

Use the ladder around that flagship:

- Choose **AI Team Briefing** when leadership needs a low-friction baseline before committing team time.
- Choose **Practical AI Workshop** when the team needs hands-on confidence and practical habits.
- Choose **AI Adoption Day** when the team wants workshop plus workflow mapping and a roadmap.
- Choose **AI Adoption Sprint** when there are priority workflows that need structured follow-through.

## Audience routes

### Teams and organisations
Use the main ladder: Briefing, Practical AI Workshop, Adoption Day, or Adoption Sprint. The Practical AI Workshop remains the flagship starting point when informal AI use is already happening.

### Training providers and partners
Use the same formats as delivery capacity: briefing, specialist module, half-day workshop, full-day session, or scoped sprint support. Commercials depend on preparation, branding, materials, IP boundaries, and delivery model.

### Nonprofits and community organisations
Use the same ladder, usually starting with a briefing or workshop. Community-access scoping can be discussed privately where budget or mission context requires it.

### Individual professionals
Use the separate learning-labs lane. These are per-seat or clinic-style professional-development offers and should not be presented as a replacement for team adoption work.

## Inclusions and boundaries

- Standard workshop pricing assumes up to **20 participants**. Larger groups should be split into cohorts or priced separately.
- Standard delivery includes a scoping call, light tailoring, role-based examples, participant handout, responsible-use checklist, use-case capture sheet, and short next-step summary.
- Deeper tailoring, sector-specific materials, multiple stakeholder interviews, or custom implementation support should be scoped separately.
- Onsite delivery may require travel/time costs depending on location and format.

## What Verdantia does not lead with

- Generic “AI automation agency” positioning.
- Vague “AI transformation” claims.

- Fake scale, invented case studies, client logos, testimonials, or over-polished consultancy theatre.

Verdantia can support workflow and automation conversations later, but the first step is making the team’s work, risks, and AI habits clear.

## FAQ / buyer objections

**Do participants need paid AI tools?**
No. The workshop can use the tools the organisation already approves. If live tool use is restricted, Verdantia can teach from prepared examples, screenshots, and safe demonstration material.

**Is this Microsoft Copilot-specific?**
It can be, but it does not have to be. Verdantia works across Microsoft Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and approved organisational tools. The focus is better workplace use, not tool fandom.

**Can this be tailored to our team?**
Yes. The standard workshop includes light tailoring through a scoping call. Deeper tailoring, sector-specific examples, or custom materials can be priced separately.

**How do you handle privacy and sensitive data?**
Participants are not asked to paste sensitive client, employee, financial, or confidential information into public AI tools. Exercises can use synthetic, sanitised, or approved internal examples.

**Is this too basic for people already using AI?**
The session is designed for mixed-confidence teams. Confident users get better structure, review habits, and workflow thinking; newer users get a safe baseline.

**What happens after the workshop?**
The team leaves with reusable materials and recommended next steps. If useful, Verdantia can provide a follow-on use-case debrief, an Adoption Day, or a focused Adoption Sprint.
